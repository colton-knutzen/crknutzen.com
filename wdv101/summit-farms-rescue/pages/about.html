About Alaskan Dogs

Alaskan Husky

Alaskan Husky's are medium-sized dogs and the most common breed for sled dog racing. Their color of coat can range from white, black, brown, and any mix-match with spots. This bred values speed over strength.

Alaskan Malamute

Alaskan Malamute are a larger breed and thus known for their strengh and endurance when it comes to pulling heavy loads. They have a thick coat to get them warm in the cold enviornments, but that means more sheading than other breeds. They are identified by their fluffy, upwards pointing, question mark-shaped tail.

Alaskan Klee Kai

The Alaskan Klee Kai is a bred breed that comes in a variety of sizes ranging from 5-22 pounds. They were bred to resemble the sled dog style, but to be more of a companion and less of a work dog.

American Eskimo Dog

The American Eskimo is another companion breed rather than work dog. They come in three sizes, but are typically smaller than the Alaskan Klee Kai. They have a thick, almost round white coat that is extra soft to the touch. They even have a bit of wolf in them!

Siberian Husky

The Siberian Husky is one of the most well known northern bred of dog. They are similar to the Alaskan Husky, but are generally smaller. They have a thick double coat and tall triangular ears.

Samoyed

Samoyed dogs are of the medium size and preform better with herding tasks rather than sled-pulling. They are typically white with a thick, poffy coat.

Greenland

The Greenland dog is another large, sled-pull breed. This breed gets it's name from their home; Greenland. Which considers them a national treasure. They love them so much the government chips and registers every Greenland dog, and it's even illegal to import other dog breeds in!
