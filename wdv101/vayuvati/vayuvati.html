Vāyuvatī Monastery

"Oh, Vāyuvatī, my dear home. The land where monks walk with wind and peace, seeking all forms of personal growth, and helping nurture others." - The Saraswadevi

The Five Dala

Vāyuvatī is a 5-peaked mountain range hidden above the Himalayas mountains. It is the Lilonon of wind and nurture. The 5 peaks float upside down in the air, held up by the Guardian Winds. Uttara-dala is the north mountain. The south is Daksine-dala. The west, Pashcima-dala. While the destoryed mountain Purva-dala rests in the east. The central is Suci-dala, the capital.

The Eight Right Paths

The Eight Right Paths are a set of eight norms by which the monks of Vāyuvatī live. Most of them are similar to basic human norms, but they are placed front and center in this culture because they believe by following them put them on the right path towards spiritual enlightenment. The Eight Paths are:

  1. Seek the truth, and do not spread lies
  2. Renounce negative thoughts of the mind
  3. Say or do nothing that harms others
  4. Work for the good of others
  5. Respect the Vessels of all forms of Chi
  6. Resist Amma
  7. Practice meditation
  8. Control your thoughts

Guardian Winds

The Guardian Winds are the life of Vāyuvatī. They are its teacher and protector. The Saraswadevi is the master of the Guardian Winds.
